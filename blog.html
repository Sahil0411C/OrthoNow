ACL Surgery Recovery Guide

Published by OrthoNow | Orthopaedic Care

Introduction

ACL (Anterior Cruciate Ligament) injuries are among the most common knee injuries in athletes and active individuals. An ACL tear can occur during sports involving sudden stops, jumping, or changing direction quickly. Surgery is often recommended for complete ligament tears to restore knee stability and function.

Recovery after ACL surgery is a gradual process requiring patience, physiotherapy, and regular follow-up with your orthopaedic surgeon. Most patients return to normal activities within several months when following the recommended rehabilitation plan.

The first few weeks after surgery focus on reducing swelling and restoring knee movement. As healing progresses, strengthening exercises and balance training help rebuild muscle power and improve stability.

Symptoms

Common symptoms include:

  • Sudden popping sound during injury
  • Severe knee pain
  • Swelling within a few hours
  • Difficulty walking
  • Knee instability
  • Reduced range of motion
  • Difficulty climbing stairs

If left untreated, repeated instability episodes may damage cartilage and meniscus, increasing the risk of arthritis later in life.

Treatment

Treatment depends on age, activity level, and severity of injury.

  • Physical examination
  • MRI Scan
  • X-Ray
  • Pain medication
  • Ice therapy
  • Knee brace
  • ACL reconstruction surgery
  • Physiotherapy

Arthroscopic ACL reconstruction is performed using a small camera and surgical instruments through tiny incisions. A tendon graft is used to replace the torn ligament.

Recovery

Recovery usually follows these stages:

Week 1–2

Control swelling, reduce pain, and begin gentle knee movement exercises.

Week 3–6

Improve walking, increase flexibility, and strengthen thigh muscles.

Month 2–4

Begin cycling, balance exercises, and moderate strengthening.

Month 5–6

Return to jogging and sports-specific rehabilitation.

Month 7–9

Return to sports after surgeon approval.

Following your rehabilitation program consistently gives the best chance of full recovery and reduces the risk of reinjury.

Conclusion

ACL reconstruction has excellent success rates when combined with proper physiotherapy. Early diagnosis, expert surgical care, and disciplined rehabilitation help patients regain confidence and return to an active lifestyle.
